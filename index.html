Gofer

What is it?

Gofer is a jQuery plugin for instant and seamless page transitions. The only thing you absolutely need for it to work is a browser that supports history.pushState and sessionStorage, however it works best when at least some of the following are true:

  1. You have a decent idea where your users might navigate to next.

    If you have something a list of links like "Recent Posts" or "Related Articles", Gofer is probably right for you. The whole idea is that if, in general, users are using your site in a predictable manner, do them a favor and fetch the content they want before they ask for it.

  2. Your pages have a similar HTML structure.

    Gofer works by selecting elements with jQuery and replacing their contents. For instance on a blog, the post content might be found in <article class=".blog-post">. This is perfect, since if we're looking for another post, we can go get a page and look for the content in the element(s) on that page that match article.blog-post.

  3. Your users hate waiting.

    They do.
